## 2. Gold derivation (the hard part)

GAPS ships per piece three files under `$TABVISION_DATA_ROOT/gaps/`:
`musicxml/<stem>.xml` (score with a TAB part carrying `<string>`/`<fret>`),
`midi/<stem>.mid` (high-resolution note-level performance MIDI), and
`syncpoints/<stem>.json` (`[measure_index, downbeat_seconds[, tempo]]` anchors).
**None alone yields onset-timed tab:** the MusicXML tab has `(string, fret)` but
only *score* time; the MIDI has exact performance onsets but no string/fret; the
syncpoints map score measures to seconds only per-measure (≈25 ms median, but
p95 70–900 ms under rubato — too coarse for the 50 ms onset gate).

The recon spike's optimistic "match MIDI onsets to score tab within onset
clusters" assumed one clean clip (`tw1wc`). The full corpus is materially
messier; the validated method (`cache/gaps_recon/gold_probe_v2.py`):

1. **Walk the TAB part** (the part whose notes carry `<string>`), honouring
   `<backup>`/`<forward>`/`<chord>` so each note gets a correct absolute *score*
   onset; read `<staff-tuning>` so **scordatura** pieces validate
   (`our_string_idx = 6 − musicxml_string`, `open_pitch` from staff-tuning).
2. **Warp** each note's score onset → approximate seconds via per-measure
   syncpoint interpolation.
3. **Snap to the exact MIDI onset.** Per pitch, monotonically align the score
   notes (warp-time order) to the MIDI notes (time order) **maximizing matched
   pairs** (gaps cost ≫ any time term), using the warp only to place the
   unavoidable repeat/ornament gaps. Each matched note takes the MIDI note's
   exact onset+duration; its `(string, fret)` comes from the score.

Three findings drove the design (each broke a simpler approach):
- **Voice-major document order.** MusicXML lists notes voice-by-voice
  (`<backup>` between), not by time — a naïve pitch-sequence LCS scored only 73%;
  sorting by computed score time lifted it to 88%.
- **Scordatura.** Several clips are drop-D etc.; assuming standard tuning mis-fired
  `(string,fret)→pitch`. Reading `<staff-tuning>` fixed it (bad-pitch → 0).
- **Repeats/ornaments.** Performances repeat sections the written score lists
  once (MIDI up to 2.3× the score), so the gold under-covers the performance →
  handled by the §5 coverage filter, not silently.

Match rate vs the per-pitch multiset ceiling (~94%): **~90% median** on
standard-tuning clips, with **exact MIDI onsets**. The production parser is
byte-identical to the validated probe on 4/5 spot-checked clips (a 2-event
chord-boundary difference on the 5th).

## 3. Result — GAPS audio is in-domain (the go/no-go)

highres (`guitar-gaps.pth`) transcribed 8 clean standard-tuning clips, scored vs
the derived gold (audio-only, no position prior):

| | onset F1 | pitch F1 | tab F1 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| mean (8 clean clips) | **0.952** | **0.946** | 0.768 |
| best (179_pM1wc) | 0.995 | 0.995 | 0.857 |

This clears the SPEC §1.4 audio gates (onset ≥ 0.92, pitch ≥ 0.90) and confirms
the recon's expectation: unlike UT-Austin (raw pitch ≈ 0, out-of-domain), GAPS
audio is **in-domain** because highres is the GAPS-lineage model. The corpus is
viable.

## 5. Per-clip quality filter (no silent caps)

Two filters, both logged:

- **Standard-tuning** (in `scan_gaps`): **22 of 30** official test clips use
  standard tuning; **8 scordatura clips dropped** (drop-D etc.) — their gold
  `(string,fret)` is unreachable by the standard-tuning pipeline.
- **Coverage** = gold / performed-MIDI notes (FP-deflation risk from
  repeats/ornaments the score-centric gold can't cover). **12 of 22** clips are
  ≥ 80% coverage (faithful gold); **10 flagged < 80%** and excluded from the
  clean headline:

| Bucket | Clips |
|---|---|
| clean (≥80%) | 027 031 043 063 104 118 142 179 212 235 294 341 |
| flagged (<80%, repeat/ornament) | 019 111 112 126 201 222 247 270 291 358 |

The error decomposition over all 22 makes the artifact explicit and separates it
from the real signal:

| Bucket | Share of loss | Reading |
|---|---:|---|
| extra_detection | 51.5% | repeat/ornament traversals absent from score-centric gold (the §5 coverage artifact) |
| **wrong_position_same_pitch** | **34.1%** | **the audio string-resolution limit — the v1.1 video lever** |
| missed_onset | 8.1% | — |
| pitch_off | 5.6% | — |
| timing_only | 0.7% | — |

After the coverage artifact, the dominant residual is exactly
`wrong_position_same_pitch` — right pitch, wrong string — which is what the
fretting-hand video chain (chunks 1–3) is built to resolve.

The recon's "implied chunk-5" had five items. Done here: dataset access (full HF
mirror, local), the parser, audio-in-domain confirmation, and the quality
filter. The gold-derivation (items 2/4) was the bulk — far larger than the

acquisition + real-chain (item 3 + the gated eval) is the remaining half (§7).

## 7. Remaining: video real-chain (scoped, not yet executed)

GAPS ships **no local video** — it requires yt-dlp from the per-clip `yt_id`
(in the metadata CSV). Feasibility **confirmed**: yt-dlp 2026.06.09 reaches
YouTube here (format 134/360p resolved for a clean clip). The non-trivial new
piece is **video↔audio offset alignment**: the GAPS audio is a *crop* of the
YouTube upload (`duration` ≠ `cropped_duration`), so frame time ≠ gold/audio
time. Plan: cross-correlate the GAPS audio against the downloaded video's audio
to recover the crop offset, map gold onsets → frame time, then run the chunk-3
confidence-gated CV chain (YOLO-OBB + MediaPipe + homography + fusion) over the
clean-12 clips and score video-assisted Tab F1 vs the 0.94 acceptance bar. This
is network + CPU heavy and is the next focused chunk.
